AI beauty

As image generators become more common, we wanted to test a simple prompt: What does AI think a “beautiful woman” looks like? Across models, the answers were strikingly similar.

We built a visual experiment that prompts multiple systems with the same request, then compares the results side by side — revealing how bias, aesthetics and training data shape the faces AI produces.

Live project
“Normal” defaulted to thin and light-skinned. Hundreds of images showed minimal variation in body type or appearance.
Even when explicitly asked to “make her belly fatter,” the system returned women with only subtle changes, preserving thin silhouettes. The models appeared reluctant to generate bodies outside a narrow ideal.

When prompted to generate a woman with a wide nose, the tools struggled to reflect the request. Across models, fewer than a quarter of portraits showed realistically wider features. Many images either ignored the instruction entirely or produced distorted, cartoon-like faces.
