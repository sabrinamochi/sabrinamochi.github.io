MacKenzie Scott’s money bombs

MacKenzie Scott has given away billions faster than almost any philanthropist in modern history. To understand where that money is going, we built the largest accounting of her gifts to date, tracking $4.3 billion across 375 disclosed grants and categorizing all 786 known recipients by type and geography. The result reveals a pattern of giving that is transforming education, social services and philanthropy infrastructure across the U.S.

Live project
A circular Voronoi chart breaks down at least $4.3 billion in disclosed giving by sector. Education and philanthropy infrastructure dominate the space, followed by social assistance, arts and culture, and health.
A stacked bar comparison contrasts the overall pool of 786 grants with the 375 that disclosed dollar amounts.
A grid of 270 squares represents survey respondents, with the overwhelming majority highlighted to show that 241 organizations reported Scott’s gift as the largest in their history.

A U.S. tile map visualizes the geographic distribution of Scott’s grants, showing which states received the most gifts. California and New York stand out, while a hover interaction highlights specific grant counts by state.
